Overview

The Set Up

The concert is broken up into 10 different eras spanning across 18 years of music. Instead of just the common "outfit change," the, set, outfits, props, and overall stage aesthetic changes completely to reflect the prospective album- or rather referred to as- era. The concert set up is completed by backup dancers and singers, a live band, and graphics on both the stage and backdrop. There are lots of lights, steam, and fire to complete the experience. One era's lights are so strong that people cities away can see them.

Some Notable Stage Sets

The Lover House

The Lover House is a X-Ray view of a fictional house that was brought up during Swift's music video for the title track "Lover." The house features different colored rooms representing her seven different albums at the time of the Lover release. In the background on tour, the house burns down, symbolizing the end of that era of love as she once knew it.

The folklore Cabin

The folklore Cabin is a mystical set piece in which she kicks off the folklore set while sitting atop the roof. It's covered with moss and decorated with delicate lighting, adding to the folklore experience.

Champagne Problems Piano

The Champagne Problems Piano is, to put it simple, a piano that Swift sits down at and performs evermore's "Champagne Problems." Similar to the folklore Cabin, it is decorated with moss and is the scene of the tour-iconic "champagne problems ovation," which has once lasted over 8 minutes.

Records Broken

The Eras Tour hasn't just been widely talked about, it has been a record-breaker. Accomplishments include:

Most shows at Wembley Stadium in a single tour

Most tickets sold in a single day

Hightest-grossing tour of all time (estimated $2.165 billion)

Highest-grossing concert movie of all time ($37 million on the first day)

Highest opening night attendees than any other female artist in the country

Among other accomplishments, The Eras Tour also boosted the economy and broke many attendance records at handfulls of stadiums.
