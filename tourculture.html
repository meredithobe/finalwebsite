Explore the Action Both Within the Duration of the Show and Beyond

"So Make the Frienship Bracelets"

A practice among fans along this tour has been exchanging handmade bracelets representing different albums and lyrics, which has created a vibrant culture of connection and community. This ritual stemmed from a lyric in Swift's song "You're On Your Own, Kid," and it enhances the concert experience, allowing Swifties to express their love for Taylor while forging friendships and sharing unforgettable memories.

Fan Chants

During the show, fans participate in spirited chants that resonate throughout the venues, creating an electric atmosphere. From iconic lyrics to catchy phrases, these synchronized outbursts showcase the collective energy and passion of Swifties, turning each concert into a communal celebration of Taylor's music and legacy. Some of the most popular include "1, 2, 3, let's go b****" during "Delicate," and "you forgive, you forget, but you never let it go" in "Bad Blood."

Thirteen Hands

Writing "13" on the back of their hands has special significance for Swifties, symbolizing their connection to Taylor. The trend was started by Taylor herself, and isn't just exclusive to the Eras Tour, as it has been a practice spanning over many years. The number 13 is her favorite and also represents themes of luck, creativity, and community. Fans often use it as a way to show solidarity and express their love for Taylor, creating a sense of unity during her concerts.

The 22 Hat

At the start of the Red era, Taylor wears a black rimmed hat while singing "22." During the song, she and her dancers make their way toward to front of the stage where a lucky fan is awaiting Taylor to give them the aforementioned hat and share a special moment together. Most often the fan is a child, but it does range in age and gender. Oftentimes the fan will "trade" for the hat by slipping a frienship bracelet onto Taylor's wrist.

Surprise Song O'Clock

Surprise songs are a thrilling highlight of the Eras Tour, where Taylor performs unexpected tracks that aren't part of the main setlist. These songs often include fan favorites, deep cuts, or even rare gems, making each concert unique. The element of surprise adds excitement and anticipation, which has made the surprise song section of the show a highly anticipated one of each stop. Fans not at the shows even tune in to live streams during this time to find out what Swift has up her sleeve for that particular night. Swift often invites a muscial guest to perform with her. Among these guests include Ed Sheeran, Sabrina Carpenter, and Paramore's Haley Williams.

The Fearless Heart

The Fearless heart hands started many years ago on Swift's original Fearless tour. It happens during the song "Fearless," where Taylor and fans share a moment showing their love to each other by throwing up a heart above their heads with their hands. Many fans describe the moment like being in a stadium full of strangers turned friends.

Light Up Wristbands

Fans are given specially-coded wristbands upon entering the stadium that create an interactive environment for fans during the show. These wristbands are programmed to respond to the show in a purposeful way to add to each set, song, and moment. The wristbands of the crowd band together to turn the stands into a canvas of moving color.
