AN INTRO TO THE ERAS

The Eras Tour by Taylor Swift is a concert production that showcases her musical evolution across different phases of her career. Each "era" represents a specific album and its unique themes, styles, and visuals. From her country roots to the pop anthems and the introspective vibes of sister albums "Folklore" and "Evermore," the tour celebrates her diverse artistry. The show features elaborate staging, costumes, and setlists that reflect the distinct identity of each album, allowing fans to experience the breadth of her work in a vibrant and immersive way. She takes the audience through these years of her musicality, one era at a time.

The eras shown below all have multiple different outfit options (with the exception of Reputation) and complex sets that complete and enrich the concert experience. Photos are shown according to album release order, not according to set order. The show opens up with the Lover (2019) era and closes out with the Midnights (2022) era. The only album of Swift's that doesn't have a set is her 2006 debut album, "Taylor Swift."

Fearless Era

Songs performed include hits "You Belong With Me" and "Love Story"

Speak Now Era

There is only one song in this era: "Enchanted." She added fan-favorite "Long Live" after the release of Speak Now TV, but took it off a few months later.

Red Era

"I Knew You Were Trouble," "We Are Never Ever Getting Back Together," and "All Too Well (10 Minute Version)" are included in this era.

1989 Era

2014 smash hits "Shake it Off," "Bad Blood," and "Blank Space" all make an appearance during this set.

Reputation Era

Taylor hisses her hits "Delicate," "Look What You Made Me Do," and fan-fave "Don't Blame Me."

Lover Era

The show opens up with the fitting lyrics "it's been a long time coming" and dives into the summer anthem "Cruel Summer," along with "The Man," "Lover," and "You Need to Calm Down."

Folklore Era

One of Taylor's longest sets includes the songs "cardigan," "my tears ricochet," "august," and "illicit affairs."

Evermore Era

The sister set features songs "willow," "champagne problems," and the emotional tribute to her late grandmother "marjorie."

Midnights Era

The lengthy closing set dazzles with her hit "Anti-Hero," along with "Bejeweled" and "Karma."

The Tourtured Poets Department Era

This newly-added set for the April 2024 release features songs "So High School," "Down Bad," and "I Can Do It With a Broken Heart."
